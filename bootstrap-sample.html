Giraffe Facts

The West African giraffe isn’t a heavy drinker. In the wet season they are able to obtain a majority of their water intake through the leaves they consume, and in the drier months, they drink a few times per week — sometimes up to 38 liters at a time.
Giraffes are listed as vulnerable on The International Union for Conservation of Nature Red List of endangered species, with population declines of up to 40 percent in the last 30 years.
Before World War I, West African Giraffes lived throughout the Sahel and Savanna regions of West Africa. However, due to extreme population decrease, they now inhabit only a small part of Niger.
Facts supplied by African Wildlife Foundation
